Yes, with the right ingredients, you CAN have cookies for breakfast.
Here we've mixed homemade granola, pumpkin seeds, coconut and flax, and turned it into a breakfast cookies recipe that helps you take on the day well-fed
and with a smile on your face :) .

    Ingredients:
  • 500g large-flake oats
  • 2 tsp. flaxseed/chia seeds
  • 30ml melted unsalted butter
  • a pinch of kosher salt
  • 1 egg white
  • 3 tsp. sweetener
    Add into the batch (options) :
  • Pecans, almonds, walnuts, coconut, pistachios, macadamia, cashews and other kinds of nuts.
  • Pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds and other kinds of seeds.
  • Ground cinnamon, ground ginger (use half as much) or pumpkin spice blends.
  • Vanilla extract.
  • Dried fruit, such as cranberries, apples, raisins, apricots, mangoes, cherries, raisins.
  • Chocolate (all kinds)
    Here’s how to do it:
  1. POSITION rack in centre of oven, then preheat. Line a large baking sheet with parchment.
  2. TOSS oats with the toppings (if you use chocolate, add it in the final batch), flaxseed/chia seeds, butter and salt in a medium bowl until well coated. Spread on prepared sheet.
  3. BAKE until the mixture smells fragrant, 10 to 12 min. (It’s okay if mixture isn’t toasted and golden-brown. It will continue to toast as it bakes in the cookies.) Remove from oven. Let cool.
  4. BEAT egg white in a medium bowl using an electric mixer until foamy, about 1 min. Beat in sugar until soft peaks form, about 2 min. Fold in the baked mixture until evenly coated.
  5. PACK portions onto same baking sheet. With damp hands, lightly shape and press the portions.
  6. BAKE, rotating sheet halfway through, until edges are golden and crisp, 20 to 25 min. Let cool 10 min on sheet, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Cookies will keep well in a sealed container on the counter for up to 5 days.
