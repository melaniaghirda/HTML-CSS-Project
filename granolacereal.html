Do you love granola? Me, too! Today, I’m sharing my “basic” granola recipe, which is also the best granola recipe.
This granola makes a wonderful snack or breakfast (add your milk of choice and maybe some fresh fruit). It also stores beautifully, so it makes a great homemade gift.
Once you try homemade granola, you won’t go back to store-bought granola. It’s so much better!

    Ingredients:
  • 500g old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 tsp. unrefined coconut oil/extra-virgin olive oil
  • honey or pure maple syrup
  • a pinch of kosher salt
    Options:
  • Pecans, almonds, walnuts, coconut, pistachios, macadamia, cashews and other kinds of nuts.
  • Pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds and other kinds of seeds.
  • Ground cinnamon, ground ginger (use half as much) or pumpkin spice blends.
  • Vanilla extract.
  • Dried fruit, such as cranberries, apples, raisins, apricots, mangoes, cherries, raisins.
    *For fresh citrus flavor, stir fresh citrus zest (up to 2 teaspoons) into the mixture before baking.
    *You can add chocolate chips after the granola has completely cooled (otherwise, they’ll melt).
    Here’s how to do it:
  • Step 1.
    Preheat the oven.
  • Step 2.
    Stir oats, nuts and seeds together in a large bowl.
  • Step 3.
    Combine honey (or maple syrup), oil and the spices in a small saucepan; Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  • Step 4.
    Stir the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and mix thoroughly;
    Transfer to a large roasting pan and bake, stirring once or twice, until golden brown, about 1 1/2 hours.
    Cool completely before stirring in dried fruit.
    Serve immediately or store in an airtight container for up to 1 month.
